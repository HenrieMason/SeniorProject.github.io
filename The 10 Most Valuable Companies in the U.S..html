Project Overview

This project focuses on analyzing the ten American companies with the highest market capitalization. These companies are Microsoft, Apple, NVIDIA, Alphabet, Amazon, Meta Platforms, Berkshire Hathaway, Eli Lilly, Tesla, and Broadcom. Please note that the top ten rankings may change over time. For the most up-to-date list, you can visit this website: companiesmarketcap.com. Only American companies were selected for this analysis due to the availability of their public financial information.

This project was completed as part of my Senior Data Science Project at BYU-Idaho. I chose this topic to integrate my finance minor with my Data Science major, providing a comprehensive solution that leverages both areas of study.

For each company, the analysis includes:

  1. Overview: A brief description of the company.
  2. Key Products and Services: Information on their main offerings.
  3. Financial Performance Snapshot: A summary of their financial health.
  4. Analysis: Examination of three key financial graphs:
  • Revenue and Cost of Revenue: Demonstrates the efficiency of their product management.
  • Assets and Liabilities: Illustrates the company’s ownership and financial obligations.
  • Cash Flow Activities: Reveals how the company allocates its financial resources.

It’s difficult to understand the cash flows activities graph so here’s a quick explanation:

  • Operating Activities: Negative values would indicate that the core business operations are not generating enough cash to cover expenses. However, in every case, the operating activities are positive, meaning each company is generating cash from its operations.

  • Investing Activities: Negative values (which you see consistently) indicate that the company is spending money on investments, such as purchasing property, equipment, or other long-term assets. This is typically seen as a good sign of growth and expansion, but it also means cash is flowing out.

  • Financing Activities: Negative values indicate that the company is spending money to repay debt, buy back shares, or pay dividends. Positive values would indicate raising funds through borrowing or issuing new shares.

  • Combined Cash Flow: This is the sum of operating, investing, and financing activities. When the combined cash flow is negative, it means that the total outflows (spending) exceed the total inflows (receiving). This can be due to heavy investments, significant debt repayments, or lower operational cash inflows.

# Set the index
microsoftI.set_index("MSFT_income-statement_Annual_As_Originally_Reported", inplace=True)

# Convert to numeric values (coerce errors)
microsoftI = microsoftI.apply(pd.to_numeric, errors='coerce')

# Transpose to switch columns and rows for easier plotting
df = microsoftI.transpose()

# Plotting
plt.figure(figsize=(9, 6))

# Plot Total Revenue
plt.plot(df.index, df["Total Revenue"] / 1e9, label="Total Revenue", color="blue")

# Plot Cost of Revenue
plt.plot(df.index, df["Cost of Revenue"] / 1e9, label="Cost of Revenue", color="red")

# Fill area beneath Cost of Revenue
plt.fill_between(df.index, df["Cost of Revenue"] / 1e9, color='red', alpha=0.3)

# Fill area between Total Revenue and Cost of Revenue (Gross Profit)
plt.fill_between(df.index, df["Cost of Revenue"] / 1e9, df["Total Revenue"] / 1e9, color='green', alpha=0.3, label="Gross Profit")

# Calculate Net Income and add annotations
net_income = df["Total Revenue"] - df["Cost of Revenue"]
for i in range(len(df.index)):
    net_income_billion = net_income.iloc[i] / 1e9
    cost_of_revenue_billion = df["Cost of Revenue"].iloc[i] / 1e9
    total_revenue_billion = df["Total Revenue"].iloc[i] / 1e9
    plt.annotate(f'{round(net_income_billion)}', 
                 (df.index[i], (df["Total Revenue"].iloc[i] + df["Cost of Revenue"].iloc[i]) / 2 / 1e9),
                 textcoords="offset points", 
                 xytext=(0,10), 
                 ha='center', 
                 fontsize=8, 
                 color='green')
    plt.annotate(f'{round(cost_of_revenue_billion)}', 
                 (df.index[i], cost_of_revenue_billion),
                 textcoords="offset points", 
                 xytext=(0,10), 
                 ha='center', 
                 fontsize=8, 
                 color='red')
    plt.annotate(f'{round(total_revenue_billion)}', 
                 (df.index[i], total_revenue_billion),
                 textcoords="offset points", 
                 xytext=(0,10), 
                 ha='center', 
                 fontsize=8, 
                 color='blue')

# Adding titles and labels
plt.title('Revenue and Cost of Revenue for Microsoft')
plt.xlabel('Year')
plt.ylabel('Amount (in billions)')
plt.legend()

# Display the plot
plt.show()

Microsoft’s total revenue has shown a significant and consistent increase from 126 billion in 2019 to 212 billion in the TTM period. TTM means “Trailing Twelve Months”, so TTM in this graph would signify the preformance from June 2023 to June 2024.

The gross profit has grown substantially, indicating effective cost management. Gross profit increased from 83 billion in 2019 to 146 billion in the TTM period.

While the cost of revenue has increased (from 43 billion in 2019 to 71 billion in the TTM period), it has grown at a slower rate compared to the total revenue, contributing to the higher gross profit.
